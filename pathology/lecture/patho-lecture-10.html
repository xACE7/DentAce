A fracture is defined as a loss of bone integrity that results from a mechanical injury or reduced bone strength. The process of restoring the injured bone to its original condition is known as fracture healing, which is a complex and sequential series of events.

Fractures can be classified based on different criteria:

  • By Cause:
    • Traumatic: Caused by a direct injury or force.
    • Pathological: Occurs in a bone weakened by an underlying disease, such as osteoporosis or a tumor.
  • By Shape:
    • Linear: A fracture that runs parallel to the long axis of the bone.
    • Spiral: A fracture where at least one part of the bone has been twisted.
    • Transverse: A straight break across the bone.

Fracture healing can occur in two main ways, depending on the stability of the fracture site:

  • This is the most common type of natural healing (e.g., when treated with a cast).
  • It involves the formation of a callus, which is a bridge of new tissue that forms around the fracture.
  • The callus forms from both the periosteum (external) and medullary cavity (internal).
  • This process results in a strong healing union.
