The Autonomic Nervous System (ANS) is a division of the Peripheral Nervous System (PNS) that controls involuntary bodily functions. These include heart rate, blood pressure, digestion, and glandular secretions. It is not under our conscious control.

The ANS is divided into two main branches that work in opposition to each other to maintain the body's internal balance, or homeostasis.

  • Sympathetic Division: This prepares the body for stressful or emergency situations, often called the "fight or flight" response.
  • Parasympathetic Division: This division dominates during times of rest and relaxation, managing routine activities like digestion. It's known as the "rest and digest" system.

Adrenergic drugs have widespread effects throughout the body:

Adrenergic drugs are used for several key therapeutic purposes:

  1. Vasoconstriction: This is a primary use in dentistry. Drugs like epinephrine are added to local anesthetics (e.g., lidocaine, articaine). This constricts local blood vessels, which:

Adverse reactions are typically an extension of the drug's intended effects, including anxiety, tremors, tachycardia (fast heart rate), and increased blood pressure.
